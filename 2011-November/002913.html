> > No, that's not a bug - independent of which filter you choose, new (not
> > yet downloaded, "blue" highlight) episodes and downloading (currently
> > being downloaded) episodes are always shown.
> > 
> > The idea behind this is that whenever there are new episodes, you either
> > download them or mark them as old.
> > 
> > In the same way, "Deleted" means "All, including deleted", and "All"
> > really means "Hide deleted, show the rest" but as you can see from the
> > screenshot, we are pretty limited in terms of screen space, so we chose
> > these short words.
> 
> That explanation is really useful. Two quick questions:
> 
> What does deleted really mean? From the word, I expected that these
> episodes are deleted from the device (which makes only for download
> episodes real sense (?)). Or is it just another state beside new and
> old?
> 
> I have now lots of new episodes which I did not mark as old in the
> past. Can I mark all not downloaded episodes as old? If it is not
> possible with the GUI, does it work with a wrapper shell script for
> the command line?

You can do this by updating the feeds, after that you should get a list
of new episodes - mark the ones you do not want and select "Mark as old"
in the lower left corner.

"Deleted" episodes are basically episodes that are deleted after being
downloaded, but you can also choose to delete episodes that you never
downloaded (basically to remove episodes from the "All" view - e.g. if
it's a serial podcast, you might want to mark everything as new, but you
want to listen to every podcast eventually - with "deleted" you can do
that).
