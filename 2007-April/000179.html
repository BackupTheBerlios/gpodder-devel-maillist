On Mon, 2007-04-02 at 18:42 -0400, Michel Salim wrote:
> By the way, what is the rationale of choosing the version number to be
> latest-stable-version + SVN tag, rather than upcoming-stable-version +
> SVN tag? Currently, to fit the Fedora versioning scheme (pre-release
> packages have release tag 0.upstreamtag.revision, i.e.
> 0.svn20070401.1) and make sure that RPM still sees this as newer than
> the stable 0.9.0, I have to set the version number to 0.9.1.

The reason for this is to let the package managing software choose the
correct version to upgrade. So, 0.9.0+svn is higher than 0.9.0, but also
lower than 0.9.1. This makes is possible to upgrade from 0.9.0 to all
development releases in between and after that to 0.9.0 without
problems.

It also makes sense from a user's view, as the current revision is
simply 0.9.0 _plus_ the svn changes from the specified date.
