Hello, gPodder developers and users!

Continuing our journey to improve community participation and opening up
gPodder a bit more (to hopefully increase development pace), I'd like to
appoint two new gPodder developers who now have commit access to our SVN
repository: Nick (nikosapi) and ﻿Jérôme Chabod.

Both have been active in developing gPodder for several months now, and
they know the gPodder codebase well enough, so I think it's just wise to
let them directly commit to our development repository.

Thanks to both of them for accepting the repository invitation. If you
have a patch that you want to get into gPodder, the procedure is the
same as before (gpodder-devel@ for small patches, a new ticket at
bugs.gpodder.org for bigger ones), but Nick and ﻿Jérôme are now able to
commit the patches to the repository without having to wait for me to
have some free time to review and merge the patch.
